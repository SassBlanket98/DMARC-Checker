Welcome to the Email Authentication Guide

Email authentication helps ensure your emails are delivered properly and protects your domain from being spoofed by spammers.

Benefits of proper email authentication:

  • Improved email deliverability
  • Protection against email spoofing and phishing
  • Enhanced sending reputation
  • Better visibility into email security

SPF

Sender Policy Framework specifies which mail servers are allowed to send email on behalf of your domain.

DKIM

DomainKeys Identified Mail adds a digital signature to your emails that can be verified by receiving servers.

DMARC

Domain-based Message Authentication tells receiving servers what to do with emails that fail SPF or DKIM checks.

This wizard will guide you through setting up all three protocols properly for your domain.

DMARC Setup

Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance (DMARC) tells receiving mail servers what to do when emails fail SPF or DKIM checks.

Choose your DMARC Policy

Select how strictly you want to enforce your DMARC policy:

Recommended for starting out. Emails that fail authentication will still be delivered, but you'll receive reports to monitor issues.

Intermediate policy. Emails that fail authentication may be placed in spam/junk folders. Use after monitoring with p=none.

Strictest policy. Emails that fail authentication will be rejected entirely. Only use after testing with less strict policies.

How to Add Your DMARC Record

  1. Log in to your domain registrar or DNS provider
  2. Navigate to your domain's DNS settings
  3. Add a new TXT record with the following settings:
    • Host/Name: _dmarc
    • Type: TXT
    • Value/Content: The DMARC record shown above
    • TTL: 3600 (or 1 hour)
  4. Save your changes

Email Authentication Guide Help

About This Guide

This interactive guide helps you set up proper email authentication for your domain by walking you through the configuration of SPF, DKIM, and DMARC with step-by-step instructions tailored to your email provider.

How to Use This Wizard

  1. Select your email provider to get customized setup instructions.
  2. Enter your domain to check your current authentication setup.
  3. Follow the SPF setup steps to configure your Sender Policy Framework.
  4. Set up DKIM for your domain with provider-specific instructions.
  5. Configure DMARC to specify how to handle emails that fail authentication.
  6. Verify your setup to ensure everything is working correctly.

DNS Record Changes

This wizard will guide you through making DNS changes for your domain. These changes:

  • May take up to 24-48 hours to fully propagate
  • Should be made carefully to avoid typos
  • May require access to your domain registrar or DNS provider

If you're not comfortable making DNS changes yourself, consider involving your IT department or domain administrator.

Testing Your Setup

After completing each step in the wizard, you can use the provided verification tools to check if your configuration is working properly. For comprehensive email deliverability testing, use our Email Tester tool.
